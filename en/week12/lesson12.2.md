# Lesson 12.2: Designing Apps for Good

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Format:** Design Thinking + Social Impact Programming
**Unit:** Computers and Society (Week 12-13)

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Apply design thinking to address real community problems
- Create simple programs that help others
- Understand how technology can create positive social impact
- Demonstrate empathy and user-centered design thinking

## Lesson Structure

### Problem Identification and Empathy (15 minutes)
- Identify real problems faced by students, families, or community
- Practice empathy interviews to understand user needs
- Focus on problems that could be addressed with simple technology solutions
- Choose problems that are meaningful and solvable

### Solution Design and Planning (20 minutes)
- Brainstorm technology solutions for identified problems
- Plan simple apps or programs that could help address needs
- Consider user experience and accessibility
- Create wireframes or sketches of solution ideas

### Prototype Programming (12 minutes)
- Begin programming simple prototypes of helpful apps
- Focus on core functionality rather than complex features
- Test ideas quickly and iterate based on feedback
- Celebrate attempts and learning rather than perfect solutions

### Impact Reflection and Sharing (3 minutes)
- Reflect on how technology can be used to help others
- Share solution ideas and get feedback from classmates
- Discuss responsibility that comes with technology creation

## Materials Needed
- Problem identification and empathy interview worksheets
- Solution design and wireframing templates
- Devices with Scratch Jr. for prototyping
- User persona and needs analysis materials
- Social impact examples and inspiration

## Assessment

### Formative
- Quality of empathy and user research
- Creativity and feasibility of solution ideas
- Understanding of user-centered design principles
- Reflection on social responsibility and impact

## Design Thinking Process
- **Empathize:** Understanding user needs and experiences
- **Define:** Clearly stating problems to be solved
- **Ideate:** Generating creative solution ideas
- **Prototype:** Creating testable versions of solutions
- **Test:** Getting feedback and improving designs

## Social-Emotional Learning
- Empathy and perspective-taking
- Social responsibility and civic engagement
- Collaboration and community building
- Purpose and meaning in learning and work

## Extension Opportunities
- Partner with local organizations to address real community needs
- Create more sophisticated prototypes with additional features
- Present solutions to school administrators or community leaders
- Connect with professional app developers and designers

## Vocabulary Introduced
- Design thinking
- Empathy
- User-centered design
- Social impact
- Prototype
