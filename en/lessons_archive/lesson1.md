## Lesson 1: What is Computer Science?

### Learning Objectives

1. **Understand basic concepts of computer science**: Students will understand that "computer science" refers not only to computer hardware, but is a discipline that explores how to solve problems.
2. **Recognize applications of computer science in daily life**: Students will be able to list how computer science helps us process information, communicate, and entertain ourselves.
3. **Spark learning interest**: Through interactive games and case studies, inspire students' curiosity and interest in computer science.

### Lesson Outline

#### I. Course Introduction (10 minutes)

- **Question-based introduction**: Guide students to think with questions such as "What is computer science? What is the relationship between computer science and computer games?"
- **Interactive discussion**: Invite students to share their understanding of "computer science" and computer applications they have seen in their lives.

#### II. Main Course Content (25 minutes)

1. **What is Computer Science?** (10 minutes)

   - **Definition**: Introduce that computer science is not just computer hardware, it is a discipline that studies how to solve problems, analyze data, and program.
   - **Examples**: Show several simple computer science application cases, such as weather prediction, robots, game design, etc., to help students understand the wide applications of computer science.

2. **Applications of Computer Science in Daily Life** (10 minutes)
   - Show through videos, pictures, or physical objects how computer science helps us in the following areas:
     - **Information processing**: How search engines help us find information
     - **Communication**: How to use networks for video calls
     - **Entertainment and games**: How computer science drives the development of games and animation

#### III. Interactive Session: Imagining the Future of Computer Science (10 minutes)

- **Brainstorming**: Guide students to imagine future technology, such as smart homes, intelligent robots, etc., to stimulate their imagination about future applications of computer science.
- **Sharing**: Let students share their ideas and help them realize that learning computer science might allow them to participate in creating the future.

#### IV. Course Summary and Q&A (5 minutes)
- Summarize the lesson content, review the definition of computer science and its applications in life.
- **Q&A session**: Answer students' questions and encourage them to continue exploring the world of computer science after class.

### Teaching Resources

- **Videos and pictures**: Showing practical applications of computer science in life.
- **Interactive whiteboard**: Convenient for students to answer and discuss.

### After-Class Activities

- **Discussion task**: Have students think about and record other things in their daily lives related to computer science.
