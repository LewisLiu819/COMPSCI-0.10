# Lesson 7.2: Pair Programming Partnership

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Format:** Collaborative Programming + Communication Skills
**Unit:** Problem Solving and Logic (Week 6-8)

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Work effectively with a programming partner
- Take turns as "driver" and "navigator" in pair programming
- Communicate technical ideas clearly to others
- Create better programs through collaboration than individual work

## Lesson Structure

### Introduction to Pair Programming (10 minutes)
- Explain roles: Driver (controls computer) and Navigator (guides and suggests)
- Demonstrate effective pair programming with student volunteer
- Establish ground rules for respectful collaboration
- Practice switching roles on cue

### Collaborative Programming Challenge (30 minutes)
- Partners work together on progressively challenging tasks
- Start with simple tasks, build to complex projects
- Enforce role switching every 5-7 minutes
- Teacher circulates to coach collaboration skills

### Reflection and Improvement (7 minutes)
- Partners discuss what worked well and what was challenging
- Share effective collaboration strategies with class
- Set goals for future pair programming sessions

### Celebration of Partnership (3 minutes)
- Recognize successful collaboration examples
- Acknowledge different types of contributions
- Thank partners for their teamwork

## Materials Needed
- Devices with Scratch Jr. (one per pair)
- Pair programming role cards
- Timer for role switching
- Collaboration challenge tasks
- Reflection worksheets

## Assessment

### Formative
- Quality of collaboration and communication
- Effective use of driver/navigator roles
- Willingness to share ideas and accept suggestions
- Improvement in programming outcomes through partnership

## Social-Emotional Learning
- Turn-taking and sharing
- Active listening skills
- Giving and receiving feedback
- Conflict resolution and compromise

## Communication Skills Development
- Explaining technical concepts clearly
- Asking clarifying questions
- Building on others' ideas
- Expressing disagreement respectfully

## Real-World Applications
- Professional collaboration in technology careers
- Teamwork skills applicable to all subjects
- Peer learning and teaching
- Project management and shared responsibility

## Extension Activities
- Pair programming with students from different grade levels
- Collaborative projects that extend over multiple sessions
- Teaching pair programming to younger students
- Reflection on collaboration in other school subjects
