# Lesson 13.2: Technology for Global Connections

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Format:** Cultural Exploration + Global Awareness
**Unit:** Computers and Society (Week 12-13)

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Understand how technology connects people around the world
- Appreciate cultural diversity through digital connections
- Recognize technology's role in global communication and learning
- Demonstrate respectful communication across cultural differences

## Lesson Structure

### Global Technology Exploration (15 minutes)
- Explore how technology enables global communication
- Learn about students in other countries and their technology use
- Understand how the internet connects people worldwide
- Appreciate both similarities and differences in global technology access

### Virtual Cultural Exchange (20 minutes)
- Connect with students or educators from other countries (if possible)
- Share local culture, traditions, and student work
- Learn about other cultures and educational experiences
- Practice respectful cross-cultural communication

### Global Problem-Solving Discussion (12 minutes)
- Identify global challenges that affect young people everywhere
- Discuss how technology can help address worldwide problems
- Brainstorm ways students can use technology to make positive global connections
- Consider future opportunities for international collaboration

### World Connections Reflection (3 minutes)
- Reflect on new learning about global connections
- Appreciate the power of technology to bring people together
- Set goals for respectful global digital citizenship

## Materials Needed
- World map and global technology access information
- Video conferencing setup for virtual exchanges
- Cultural sharing materials and presentations
- Global problem identification resources
- Reflection and goal-setting materials

## Assessment

### Formative
- Understanding of global technology connections
- Quality of cross-cultural communication and respect
- Engagement with global perspectives and problems
- Thoughtfulness in reflection and goal-setting

## Cultural Awareness and Appreciation
- Recognition of global diversity and commonalities
- Respectful communication across cultural differences
- Understanding of varying technology access and opportunities
- Appreciation for different perspectives and experiences

## Global Citizenship
- Awareness of worldwide challenges and opportunities
- Understanding of interconnectedness and shared responsibility
- Recognition of technology's role in global problem-solving
- Commitment to positive global digital citizenship

## Extension Opportunities
- Establish ongoing partnerships with international classrooms
- Create collaborative projects with students from other countries
- Research global technology access and digital divide issues
- Participate in international student technology competitions or exchanges

## Vocabulary Introduced
- Global connections
- Cultural exchange
- International collaboration
- Digital divide
- Global citizenship
