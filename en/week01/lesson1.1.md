# Lesson 1.1: Welcome to the Digital World

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Format:** Introduction + Exploration Activity
**Unit:** What is Computer Science? (Week 1-2)

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Define what a computer is in simple terms
- Identify at least 5 different types of computers in their environment
- Explain one way computers help people in their daily lives
- Demonstrate basic respect for classroom technology

## Lesson Structure

### Opening Circle (10 minutes)
- Welcome and introductions
- "What do you think of when you hear 'computer'?" brainstorm
- Share course expectations and classroom technology rules

### Main Activity: Computer Detective Hunt (25 minutes)
- Students work in pairs with detective clipboards
- Explore classroom/school to find and document different computers
- Categories: Traditional computers, smart devices, embedded computers
- Recording sheet with pictures and descriptions

### Closing Discussion (10 minutes)
- Share findings from detective hunt
- Create class chart of discovered computers
- Preview next lesson with excitement builder

### Technology Integration (5 minutes)
- Brief introduction to classroom devices (tablets/computers)
- Practice proper handling and basic navigation

## Materials Needed
- Detective clipboards with recording sheets
- Pencils and crayons
- Chart paper for class computer list
- Classroom devices for introduction
- Computer identification cards with pictures

## Assessment

### Formative
- Observation during detective hunt activity
- Participation in class discussions
- Completion of detective recording sheet

### Exit Ticket
Draw one computer you found today and write one sentence about how it helps people.

## Homework/Extension
Send home "Family Computer Hunt" worksheet for students to explore computers at home with families.

## Vocabulary Introduced
- Computer
- Device
- Technology
- Smart (as in smartphone, smart TV)
