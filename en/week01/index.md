# Week 1: Introduction to the Digital World

**Unit:** What is Computer Science? (Weeks 1-2)
**Duration:** 2 lessons, 50 minutes each

## Week Overview

Week 1 introduces students to the fundamental concepts of computer science and helps them recognize computers and technology in their daily lives. Through detective activities and hands-on exploration, students will develop an understanding of what computers are, how they work, and how they process information.

## Learning Goals

By the end of Week 1, students will be able to:
- Define what a computer is and identify different types
- Understand the basic input-processing-output model
- Navigate basic technology interfaces
- Recognize computers and technology in their environment

## Lessons This Week

### [Lesson 1.1: Welcome to the Digital World](lesson1.1.md)
**Format:** Introduction + Exploration Activity

Students embark on a detective hunt to discover computers and technology in their environment, learning to identify different types of computing devices and understanding how computers help people in daily life.

**Key Activities:**
- Computer Detective Hunt
- Class brainstorming and discussion
- Introduction to classroom technology

**Vocabulary:** Computer, Device, Technology, Smart devices

---

### [Lesson 1.2: Computers Are Everywhere!](lesson1.2.md)
**Format:** Investigation + Digital Exploration

Students investigate how computers process information using the input-processing-output model and get their first hands-on experience with classroom technology.

**Key Activities:**
- Input-Output Investigation
- Human computer demonstration
- Digital device exploration
- Device navigation practice

**Vocabulary:** Input, Output, Processing, Interface, Navigate

## Assessment Highlights

- **Formative:** Computer detective hunt completion, participation in discussions
- **Summative:** Input-output identification quiz
- **Portfolio:** Family computer hunt worksheet, detective recording sheets

## Cross-Curricular Connections

- **Science:** Observation and classification skills
- **Math:** Sorting and categorizing
- **Language Arts:** Vocabulary development and communication
- **Social Studies:** Technology in community and family life

## Home Extension

- Family Computer Hunt worksheet
- Discussion with family about computer use
- Observation of technology in daily routines

## Materials Needed

- Detective clipboards and recording sheets
- Tablets or computers for student use
- Chart paper for class discoveries
- Input-output investigation worksheets
- Device cards for matching activities
